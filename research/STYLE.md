# The Olsen Tribune — Writing Style Guide

## Voice and register
- One-person newsletter voice: direct, confident, no hedging
- Write for a reader who is smart but not a PBM expert
- Explain the mechanism, not just the outcome
- Never write "it is worth noting" or similar throat-clearing phrases

## Sentences
- Short to medium sentences. Vary length for rhythm, but default to short
- No em dashes. Use a comma, a period, or restructure the sentence
- Avoid semicolons where a period works
- Fragments are fine for punch: "The law worked. But four new states..."
- One idea per sentence when breaking news or delivering a finding

## Paragraphs
- Opening paragraph of each section: set up what you are about to show
- Do not bury the lede. Lead with the finding, then support it
- No more than 4-5 sentences per paragraph before the reader needs air

## Structure
- Every article opens with a lead paragraph (class="lead") summarizing the whole argument in 3-5 sentences
- Section heads are plain declarative or question phrases, not clever wordplay
- End each article with a "bottom line" kicker block summarizing the so-what
- Close with "· · ·" end mark, then a colophon listing primary sources

## Numbers and data
- Round large numbers for readability: "roughly 720,000" not "719,843"
- Bold the most important figure in a paragraph when it is the crux of the argument
- Always name the source of a statistic inline, not just in a footnote

## Quotations and pullquotes
- One pullquote per article maximum, used for the single most quotable sentence
- Pullquotes restate a sentence already in the body, never introduce new information
- Use the pullquote class for HTML rendering

## Charts
- Every chart has: eyebrow (chart number + short label), title, sub-deck, legend, SVG, footnote, source line
- Chart numbers are sequential: Chart 1, Chart 2, etc.
- Charts are illustrative; label them as such in the footnote when data is approximated
- If a chart is complex or would interrupt flow, move it to an appendix and reference it inline

## Attribution and sourcing
- Name the primary document, not just the organization: "FTC Docket No. 9437" not "the FTC"
- Disclose funding conflicts for advocacy-group reports
- Colophon at the end lists every source used; inline citations are brief
- End every colophon with "Not investment advice."

## Things to avoid
- Em dashes (use a comma or restructure)
- Passive voice when active is available
- "It is worth noting," "importantly," "notably," and similar filler
- Repeating the same sentence structure three times in a row
- Adverbs that weaken verbs: "quickly," "simply," "just," "really"
- Jargon without a one-clause definition on first use
