Charts Pack — FTC v. Express Scripts (D9437)

What the FTC Actually Locked In

Reading the Express Scripts decree, the agency didn’t blow up the pharmacy-benefit-manager model — it made it the opt-out option. Four charts on what changes, what doesn’t, and where the savings actually come from.

Chart 1 · What’s in the order
Most of the decree is opt-out
Section XI lets plan sponsors waive most provisions in writing. Only a handful of sections survive without an off-ramp.

Chart 2 · Who’s actually locked in
A small slice of ESI’s book is hard-wired to the new rules
Cigna’s own fully-insured plans cannot opt out of Section II. Self-insured employer plans — the bulk of ESI’s 123.6M pharmacy customers — can.
Locked into § II (Cigna fully-insured) Opt-out under § XI (self-insured + non-Cigna plan sponsors)
Estimate. Cigna’s 10-K and earnings disclosures don’t segment Cigna fully-insured commercial members as a percentage of ESI’s total pharmacy customer count. Chart 3 · Where the $7B comes from
Insulin’s gross-to-net bubble — and how it deflated
List prices climbed for a decade while net prices flatlined or fell. Manufacturer-led list-price cuts in 2023 closed much of the gap before the FTC ever signed.

Chart 4 · What kicks in when
A staggered rollout, not a switch flip
The headlines came in February 2026. The structural changes phase in over more than two years — and the order runs for a decade after that.
The “Implementation Date” is defined as the earlier of (i) ESI’s certification of completion or (ii) Jan 1, 2027. Term runs 10 years from Implementation Date, so expiration is 2037 absent earlier action.
Source: FTC, Decision and Order, Docket No. 9437 (Feb. 4, 2026), §§ M, V, VIII, X, XII, XVII.
Chart 5 · Who actually uses insulin
The decree only touches a fraction of insulin users
Of 7.3 million U.S. adult insulin users, only commercially-insured patients on an ESI plan are reached by Section II. Medicare and Medicaid patients are governed by separate rules.
Apportionment of insulin users by PBM uses the Big Three’s overall U.S. prescription-claim shares (ESI 30%, Caremark 27%, OptumRx 23%) as a proxy. Actual insulin-prescription share by PBM is not publicly disclosed and may differ.
Sources: HHS ASPE Insulin Affordability Report; CDC NHIS; Drug Channels “Top PBMs of 2024”; Tribune calculation.
